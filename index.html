About

Economics degree.
Product brain.

I started in economics at LUMS — decent training for product management, since both are about what people actually do under constraints, not what they say they'll do.

Since then I've worked across compliance software, supply-chain risk, and now climate tech, usually landing on the same kind of problem: a team with a lot of context and no shared direction. My job is turning that into a roadmap people can execute against.

I'm most useful early — before the PRD exists, before anyone agrees on scope — and I stay through launch to make sure what shipped actually works for the people using it.
